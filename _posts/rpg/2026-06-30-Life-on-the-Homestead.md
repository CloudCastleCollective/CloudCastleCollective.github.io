> Through your journal entries you take on the life of a homesteader.

You start with dice rolls to define starting location, skills, crop and animals that you'll be tending to. And from there drawing cards will guide you through the experience of living on a homestead.

<!--more-->

# Gameplay

### Timing
You can go on as long as you are having fun... or until you went throught the whole deck of cards!

### Tools
- 1 d6
- A standard deck of palying cards

That's it. You might want to add a notepad if you want to keep track of your adventures.

### Setup
- Homestead name: Jade's Fields

Setup rolls:
[ 1 ] - Deep in the forest
[ 1 ] - With a rich fishing lake
[ 1 ] - With a corn field
[ 5 ] - There is a lake, so it makes sense that we are tending ducks!

### Let's roll
(or draw, in this case)

- Day 0 \
Finally summer is here, and I could leave behind the office and move back to my beloved Jade's Fields. They are exactly as I left them at the end of last season... apart from the corn field that is beautifully green and some branches that grew a bit too close to home. I always love coming here to this wild meadow of flowers and herbs: I'll take care of the branches, but I'll definitely leave the yard as it is, just making a little path to get through easily.

- Day 1 - **8 of Clubs: Spend the day hiking** \
It is a beautiful day, from the yard I can see the blue sky and the sun that will pop up soon above the trees. But in the meantime I see the rays playing in the branches, shining light on the forest floor. Here and there I can see the bright green of moss, in other places the soil is releasing humidity in a very thin fog. This view is a call hard to resist: I put on shoes, some water, bread and cheese in the backpack and I get going. I'll go all around the lake, hidden right behind the embankment that can be seen between the trees, and on my way back I'll also hop over to check how the field is doing.

- Day 2- **10 of Clubs: Discover a pond** \
I was wrong after all, not everyrthing is as I left it last year. A big tree has fallen during the winter, and in the hole left by the roots there is now a little pond. The terrain must be very rich of clay, given that water remains there rather than filtering away. It does not look like the most healthy place ever, but the frogs and a wide bunch of insects seem to appreciate. I wonder if I can find a way to let the water flow a bit, at least to try and avoid mosquitoes.

- Day 3- **K of Hearts: Obtain a new animal** -> *3: Goats* \
Today the helpers will come! I can not take care of this all alone... so the team of Giustina, Guendalina, Giulietta e Giovanna will be in charge of keeping under control the shore of the lake and let me have some fresh eggs, while Anselma and Aurelia will be responsible for the meadow mowing. Last year I had attempted to do some fresh cheese with their milk, but the experiment wasn't particularly successful. I am very set on trying it again!

- Day 4- **6 of Clubs: An animal gives birth** \
I have to admit that Anselma did look a bit fatter than what she was supposed to be... like, very much fatter! This morning finding 3 goats instead of 2 was a wonderful suprise, and also explains the size issues of the mother. A bit less milk for me, but the cutest baby goat in exchange. I am happy that the birthing seems to have gone smoothly, I would really not have known how to help! And I am happy that the newborn is female, so I won't have to send it away. I wonder if like baby hair also goat hair might change color... but at the moment it seems to have a wonderful salt and pepper fur, so I'll call it Argentina.

- Day 5- **6 of Diamonds: An animal escapes the pen** \
Argentina seems to be a fitting name... Quicksilver, no jokes! She can barely stand on her own legs and today I already had to go look after her inside the woods. It might be that my carpentry skills are very poor, but when I went outside after lunchtime to put the dishes in the fountain, the pen was destroyed and there was no trace of the goatling. No trace so to say, because she was actually desperately bleating right behind the trees, and Anselma had found her no problem well before me. Regarding the pen, I suspect the issue arose just because Argentina fell on it. I wonder if trying to rebuild it would be worth it, or not really.

- Day 6- **5 of Clubs: Sustain an injury while working** \
Today the wind picked up and the sky was very cloudy, which tendentially are signs predicting an incoming thunderstorm. The motivation that I was waiting for to cut those dangerous branches near the house. Let's make this short: everything went well until the last branch, which broke when I was not expecting it. I fell from the ladder, which resulted in the saw I was handling planting itself in my arm. All in all, it is nothing too bad, but it is relatively deep and the blade wasn't exactly spotless. So, cleaning the wound with alcool that burns like hell, taking a painkiller and now lemme go to sleep because staying awake it hurts too badly!

- Day 7- **7 of Diamonds: Create an item with a learned skill** \
It is clear that I wont be able to work today: my arm looks not too bad, clean and not swollen, but it is still throbbing and if something touches it I see stars. When I checked on the corn field I had collected the material to create baskets: I thought of keeping the activity for some rainy day down the line, but I think it's something relatively light in the use of arms so I'll give it a try today. We will see if it really is any easy or not!

- Day 8- **9 of Diamonds: Days of rain causes flooding** \
After all, basketing really became a rainy day activity. After yesterday's thunderstorms, this morning sun appearance was just a break before the pouring restarted in full force. The goats are sheltered, the ducks are happy... and I am worrying. I have never seen water so high along the embankment, and the field is completely flooded. There seems to not be any place for water to drain, and if the weather keeps like this also this night I'll seriously have to consider how to prevent water seeping inside. For today, showel at hand and I go to make sure that the channels along the field are not clogged, so that when it will finally stop raining there will a chance for the water to get out of my crops!

- Day 9- **A of Diamonds: Obtain a new crop** -> *2: Potatoes* \
Thank goodness during the night it stopped raining and the level of the waters is already back to almost normal. The terrain in the woods is soft but acceptable, while the meadow and the field are sadly muddly or even swampy at places. I have hope that with a couple sunny days at least the surface should dry and the corn might recover. On the other hand, I have no idea how deep the water seeped in, and I fear that the potatoes will all end up rotting. The only thing I can do already today is making sure that plants have not been broken, and try to straighten those that have been bent.

- Day 10- **Q of Clubs: Obtain a pet** \
Everybody listen: today we had an excess of cuteness! And this is saying a lot, considering that Argentovivo is around. Anyway. Usually I do not have much food scraps, but when it happens I put it in the compost heap in a corner of the yard. It was clear to me that there was a visitor profiting of that, and today for some reason he decided to pass by in the mid-afternoon and I could see it: it is a fox with the most fluffy tail ever!!! I assume it has been observing me ever since I arrived, because when I came out of the house it did not show any fear, and actually after looking at me for a moment it calmly sat down and started cleaning itself. It is so cuteeeeeee! Luckily it is also quite small so I thik it is no danger for the goats. However I wonder if the ducks might be in danger. Until yesterday the gang was still all accounted for, but I'll have to start paying attention if I see any sign of the fox around the lake.

# Review
I very much loved this!
I put only 10 days here, but I actually went on playing for a handful more, and I'd have continued even longer.

The prompts bring just enough variety while allowing you to stick to the world that you created in your mind, and along the way I also had the opportunity to add other characters to this.
I think the structure would allow coming back to this game from time to time and just continue it: as I started, my character was there for the season, so after a closing scene I could pick up the game again and check what would be happening the next year.
