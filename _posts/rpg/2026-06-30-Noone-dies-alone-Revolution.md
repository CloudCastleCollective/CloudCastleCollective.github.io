It describes itself as a Poetry Writing Game, and I am very very curious about this!

<!--more-->

# Gameplay

### Timing
The description says about 1h, but I'd say much quicker

### Tools
- 1 d6
- A standard deck of palying cards

That's it. You might want to add a notepad if you want to keep track of your adventures.

### Setup
- Pulling 1d6 of covered cards, they will be used for composing the poem
- Pulling 4 cards for the Dawn, Noon, Dusk and Night of the Saint

### Let's roll

- Dawn - **A of Clubs** \
The Saint believe in freedom, in respecting each other in being all the same in front of the law. The universal law, not the one decided by the so-called powerful. So when people started being taken from their homes and put in camps waiting to be "sent back" to "somewhere", he knew he had to act: all people are welcome because in the universal law there are no strangers.

- Noon - **7 of Clubs** \
If the powerful dont listen, make everybody else listen. The Saint went to squares, neighborhoods, rallies, bars, theaters; where the people was he was too, to let his message known. The only law is the one of the universe, it does not matter how you show your faith to it. All the prayers will go to the same universe, all efforts will help the cause.

- Dusk - **2 of Clubs** \
The only faith that cannot be accepted is the corrupted faith of those believing in the powerful. Those that are ordered to walk the streets and take care of the people that don't belong. Not only the strangers, not anymore. Also those that embrace weird ideals. Those that stand in square arm in arm and face the tear gas. Those that face the rubber bullets at first, and then the real ones. It might have hit anyone, but it was the Saint that got hit.

- Night - **6 of Hearts** \
Everybody has heard the story of the Saint: when it got too dangerous to walk the streets, it was still told in private, spreading maybe even faster to let everybody know about what had happened. It was too dangerous to be seen taking any action, so they took actions that could not be seen. Dropping seeds of dandelions, and letting them find their way in road cracks, potholes, roadworks. They grow without any care needed, spread in the wind, blossom in very harsh conditions: the faith of the Saint is now everywhere in the streets, impossible to estirpate.

- Prayer
* Dactylic, Iambic, Dactylic, Free, Dactylic, Dactylic*

Sitting high on a tree you shouldn't cut the branches
The support is a tangle
Climbing down is too late
You are well dressed and irrelevant
They are waiting for you
Stepping on cut branches to lift themselves up

# Review
The setup allows to build a very strong figure, especially in the current times there is plenty of inspiration to create a Saint with a cause worth being faught.
Additionally, the idea of writing a prayer in the form of poetry also has a ton of potential.

But the poetry part had me lost: the guidance is in the form that the verses should take, and the tone they should have. So if you have no idea what a Dactylic is, the syllable and accent description is not going to help much. Probably even less if you are not a native English speaker.
I tried to write a little prayer, but I could have done as much without the guidelines. Too bad, I'd have loved some more accessible indications there to come up with something better.
